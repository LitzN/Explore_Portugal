NATURE HOTSPOTS

Madeira

Right in the middle of the Atlantic, the islands of Madeira and Porto Santo are a haven of natural beauty. The exotic colours of the flowers stand out from among the blue sea and the emerald green vegetation; this is an archipelago where two thirds are a protected area and where the largest Laurisilva forest in the world is located.

Monte Palace Tropical Garden, 100,000 species of plants. Monte Palace's museum contains a whole floor displaying an astonishing range of minerals, gems and diamonds.

Maderia Botanical Gardens. This beautiful scenic location, with it's large display of indigenous plants is known to attract many local colourful parrots and other wildlife.

Levada trails. A series of aqueducts used around the island is a hugely popular hiking trail, with dozens of natural beauty spots and waterfalls.

Azores

Mother Nature has created a land full of natural beauty, ready to be explored, in the blue immensity of the Atlantic Ocean: the Azores. To the East, on the island of Santa Maria, the beaches of warm white sand are inviting, and the vineyards covering the slopes like an amphitheatre resemble giant staircases. Cavern Of Coal, an ancient lava tube with it's sparkling mineral walls, is an enchanting experience. Stand inside the magma chamber of this extint volcano and look up at the sky.

Lagoa das Furnas, a crater lake with hotsprings. Local restaurants and chefs use these hotsprings for cooking, view this natural wonder and taste the local cusine.

Whale and dolphin watching and diving are just some of the exciting underwater activities avaliable in Azores.
